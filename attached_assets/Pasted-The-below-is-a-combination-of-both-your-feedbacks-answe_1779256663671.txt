Read it twice. Overall it's a well-designed split — clear value ladder, fair price ratio (1.67×, which is approachable; most SaaS uses 2-3×), and you've correctly put the practice-management + investment-focused features behind Premium where they belong. Three genuine concerns and a few smaller observations.
Two changes I'd push back on

1. Grader + temperature behind Premium feels too aggressive
The grader is what makes the lead registry actually useful vs a list of names. If a Basic advisor sees 30 leads but can't see which ones are Gold/Hot, the registry feels dumb — and they'll either churn or never upgrade because they don't know what they're missing.
My recommendation: split the grader itself.
• Basic: shows the grade badge (Gold / Silver / Bronze / Development) and temperature pill (Hot / Warm / Cold) on each lead row. They can see what the lead is.
• Premium: unlocks the full breakdown panel (income/age/lifestyle/services/source score breakdown), plus the analytics dashboard view (grade distribution over time, conversion funnel by temperature).
This way Basic still feels valuable, Premium still has a clear "why I'd pay more" — understanding why a lead is Hot vs seeing that it's Hot.
2. Digital Business Card behind Premium hurts your growth loop
The business card is the only mechanism in the entire app where a Basic user generates organic acquisition for AC. Every WhatsApp share / LinkedIn post = a free "made with Advisory Connect" footer in front of new eyeballs. Putting it behind Premium reduces share volume by ~80% (Basic will be most of the base).
My recommendation:
• Basic: gets the business card with one standard template (portrait + square) and the advisoryconnect.pro footer baked in. They share, you grow.
• Premium: unlocks multiple card variants (Stories, Feed, LinkedIn header, email signature), theme-matched colour customisation, and the ability to remove the advisoryconnect.pro footer if they want a white-label feel.
That gives you a real upsell and preserves the viral loop.
3. Enhanced grader FIELDS in Premium is the wrong axis
If the new lead-form fields (How did you find me, Net worth, Biggest concern, Existing advisor, Why referring, Have a Will, Estate value) only show on Premium advisors' public forms, your Basic advisors will permanently collect worse leads — which they'll experience as "Advisory Connect doesn't deliver quality" rather than realising it's a tier limitation.
My recommendation: put the fields in Basic (every advisor captures rich leads), put the advanced insights the fields enable in Premium (full breakdown panel, "Why this lead is Gold" explanation, source attribution analytics dashboard).
One genuine gap
My Clients tab is not mentioned in either tier. This is the biggest Premium-worthy feature in the entire roadmap — POPIA-encrypted document vault, client portal, Book of Life, birthday tracking. It needs to be explicitly listed in Premium so advisors know what they're paying for. Suggested copy: "My Clients practice management — store policy documents, ID/bank details (encrypted), Book of Life, client birthdays + follow-ups".
Smaller observations
• "Liberty-aligned risk categories" — careful with the wording. Tying your output to a competitor's brand makes you dependent on their methodology if they change it. Suggest: "industry-standard risk categories (Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive) recognised by all major SA fund houses" — same content, no brand dependency.
• "Priority support" in Premium — worth defining what it means in practice before you go live. Even a soft commitment like "24-hour response Mon-Fri vs 72-hour for Basic" gives advisors a concrete reason to value it.
• All themes + custom hex in Basic — generous choice (most SaaS would push custom hex to Premium). I think you're right to keep it in Basic — visual freedom is essential for advisor confidence in the first 14 days. Just calling it out as a deliberate generosity, not an oversight.
