Advisory Connect – Financial Industry Input Feasibility
Mapping each scoring parameter to what's realistic to collect in a financial services referral context.

The Core Challenge
Financial services has a fundamental tension: the most valuable qualification data is often the most sensitive or regulated. A referrer (e.g. an accountant, broker, or attorney) may know a lot about their client but face confidentiality, compliance, or relationship risk if they share too freely.

Parameter-by-Parameter Breakdown

1. Engagement (25% weight)
Easy to get — fully system-generated
This requires no input from the referrer at all. The platform captures it automatically.
Input	Source	Ease
Email open / link click	Platform tracking	✅ Automatic
Form completion rate	Platform	✅ Automatic
Response time to follow-up	CRM log	✅ Automatic
Repeat visits to referral portal	Analytics	✅ Automatic
No friction. No compliance risk. Pure behavioural signal.

2. Fit (20% weight)
Mostly easy — but requires some referrer input
Input	Source	Ease
Industry / sector of the lead	Referrer states on submission	✅ Easy
Company size (employee count)	Referrer or LinkedIn lookup	✅ Easy
Geographic location	Referrer states	✅ Easy
Asset class relevance	Referrer selects from dropdown	✅ Easy
Regulatory profile (e.g. FSP licensed?)	Hard to know without disclosure	⚠️ Medium
Most fit data is non-sensitive and referrers are comfortable sharing it.

3. Urgency / Timeline (15% weight)
Easy to ask, but answers are often vague
Input	Source	Ease
Timeframe to make a decision	Referrer selects (dropdown: Now / 1–3 months / 6+ months)	✅ Easy
Triggering event (e.g. business sale, retirement, divorce)	Referrer describes briefly	✅ Easy — if trust exists
Regulatory deadline driving urgency	Referrer may not know	⚠️ Medium
The trigger event is the most valuable urgency signal in financial services — a business owner selling in 90 days is gold. Referrers usually know this and will share it if the form makes it easy.

4. Authority (15% weight)
Easy to ask — but referrers sometimes don't know or won't say
Input	Source	Ease
Is the lead the decision-maker?	Yes/No toggle	✅ Easy
Role / title of the lead	Referrer states	✅ Easy
Does the lead have a spouse/partner involved?	Referrer may know	⚠️ Sensitive
Is a family office or trustee involved?	Referrer may know	⚠️ Medium
Board/committee approval required?	Referrer often doesn't know	❌ Hard
In financial services, authority is often shared (couples, trustees, boards). Single decision-maker assumptions break down here more than in other industries.

5. Budget Strength (15% weight)
The most sensitive parameter — needs careful framing
This is where financial services diverges sharply from other industries. Asking "what's the budget?" directly can feel invasive or breach referrer confidentiality obligations.
Input	Source	Ease
Investable assets (range, not exact)	Referrer selects bracket	⚠️ Sensitive but workable
Annual revenue (for business referrals)	Referrer selects bracket	✅ Easier than personal wealth
Deal size estimate	Referrer states	⚠️ Medium
Specific AUM figure	Referrer unlikely to disclose	❌ Hard
Insurance premium range	Referrer can estimate	✅ Easier
Willingness to pay (advisory fee range)	Unknown at referral stage	❌ Hard
The workaround: Use asset brackets (e.g. R1M–R5M / R5M–R20M / R20M+) rather than exact figures. Referrers in financial services are comfortable with ranges and it avoids direct disclosure of client financials.

6. Source Credibility (10% weight)
Fully system-generated over time — nothing to ask
Input	Source	Ease
Referrer's historical conversion rate	Platform calculates	✅ Automatic
Volume of referrals submitted	Platform tracks	✅ Automatic
Quality-to-volume ratio	Platform calculates	✅ Automatic
Referrer's professional designation (CFP, CA, attorney)	One-time profile setup	✅ Easy
Referrer's relationship to lead (close vs distant)	Referrer states at submission	✅ Easy
Credibility is the one factor that gets smarter over time with zero ongoing effort from the referrer.

Summary: What's Easy vs Hard
Factor	Easy Inputs	Hard/Sensitive Inputs
Engagement	All of it — automatic	Nothing
Fit	Sector, size, location	Regulatory profile
Urgency	Timeframe, trigger event	Regulatory deadlines
Authority	Role, decision-maker Y/N	Shared authority, trustees
Budget	Asset brackets, revenue range	Exact AUM, fee willingness
Source Credibility	All of it — automatic	Nothing

Key Design Recommendations for Financial Services
Use brackets, never exact figures for anything wealth-related. Referrers will answer; they won't disclose client net worth.
Add a "trigger event" field — this is uniquely powerful in financial services (retirement, business exit, divorce, inheritance, regulatory change) and referrers almost always know it.
Make authority a multi-select — "Who else is involved in this decision?" with options like Spouse, Trustee, Board, Accountant. Single-person authority is the exception, not the rule.
Frame budget questions around the deal, not the person — "Estimated deal size" lands better than "client's asset value" with referrers who have fiduciary or confidentiality obligations.
Credibility scoring should factor in professional designation — a CA or CFP referring a client carries inherently more signal than an anonymous referral, and this can be captured at onboarding with zero ongoing friction.
