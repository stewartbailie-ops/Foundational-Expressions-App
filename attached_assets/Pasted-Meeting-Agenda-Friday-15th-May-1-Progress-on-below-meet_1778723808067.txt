Security for client PII. Storing ID numbers, payslips, bank details, and policy documents puts us deep into POPIA "special personal information" territory. The minimum should be:
	• Encryption at rest for ID number, bank details, tax number — column-level AES via a key from a new PII_ENCRYPTION_KEY secret. Server decrypts on read.
	• Encrypted file storage for uploaded documents — files at rest are encrypted, only the advisor who owns the client can fetch.
	• Audit log — every read/write of sensitive PII gets a row in an audit table (who, what, when, IP). POPIA requires this.
	• Per-advisor isolation — backend queries hard-scope to clientRecord.advisorId === session.advisorId. No client document ever returnable to a different advisor.
	• Consent capture — when a lead is promoted to a client, capture explicit consent ("This advisor may store my personal documents"). Datestamp + IP.
	• Delete-on-request — POPIA right-to-erasure. We need a "permanently delete client" path that wipes encrypted data and audit-logs the deletion event itself.
	• One additional consideration: Stripe's South Africa onboarding requires a SA-registered business with a SA bank account linked — confirm those details are in order before the build session

ADVISORY CONNECT - STRATEGIC RESTRUCTURE

Advisory Connect was originally built as a tool for financial advisors, helping them manage leads, share profiles, and present themselves professionally. Today's meeting surfaced a larger opportunity: the platform can simultaneously serve the general public seeking financial guidance, not just the advisors serving them. This dual-audience approach transforms Advisory Connect from a B2B advisor tool into a full financial services ecosystem, connecting everyday South Africans with qualified advisors, while giving those advisors a world-class practice management platform.

Audience A - The General Public Individuals looking for financial advice, insurance, retirement planning, estate planning, or any financial service. They arrive with no existing advisor relationship, or want to connect with their current advisor digitally.
Audience B - Financial Advisors Registered FSPs and financial planners who need a professional digital presence, lead management, client relationship tools, and business intelligence - all in one place.
Both audiences are served through the same platform (website + app), but each has a clearly distinct entry point and journey.

The current website (www.advisoryconnect.pro) speaks only to advisors. It needs to become a dual-entry experience.
Public-facing section (default landing):
	• Explains the value to the general public: "Find a trusted financial advisor near you", "Connect with a specialist", "Manage your financial life in one place"
	• Highlights what they can do: find an advisor, link to an existing advisor, submit a query, access financial tools and education
	• Clean, accessible, jargon-free copy aimed at everyday South Africans
Advisor-facing section (separate entry, same domain):
	• Retained from the current website - explains what Advisory Connect does for advisors: lead management, digital profiles, client relationship tools, analytics
	• Reached via a clear "I'm a Financial Advisor" CTA on the landing page, or via a direct URL path (e.g. /advisors)
Website CTAs - Public Landing:
	• "Connect with an advisor" for individuals with no existing advisor
	• "Link to my advisor" for individuals who already have an advisor and want to connect digitally
Website CTAs - Advisor Entry Point:
	• "Register as an advisor"
	• "Log in to my Control Panel"
	• "Register through my organisation" (new - enterprise/white-label onboarding)

"Link to my advisor" The member of the public already has an advisor on the platform. They search by advisor name or scan a QR code (Use a shared code), and are linked directly to that advisor's public profile. From there they can submit a callback request, access their advisor's tools, or initiate the Book of Life / My Clients onboarding.
"Connect with an advisor" The member of the public has no existing advisor. They complete a short discovery form (name, contact, financial needs, location, rough income bracket). This submission enters a leads pool. Distribution options:
	• Option A (Manual): Leads land in the Master Control Panel registry, and an administrator manually assigns or routes them
	• Option B (Smart Match - future): An algorithm matches the lead against advisor profiles (location, specialisation, availability) and assigns automatically
	• Option C (Enterprise): If the user arrived via an organisation's branded link or app, the lead routes directly to that organisation's master panel and their advisor pool

This is the highest-value subscription tier, targeted at financial services organisations (Liberty, Sanlam, Discovery, Blue Asset, Envestpro, etc.).
What they get:
	• Their own branded master Control Panel (logo, colours)
	• A pool of advisor accounts 
	• A single billing relationship, one invoice to the organisation, not per-advisor
	• Lead distribution from public "Connect" submissions into their advisor pool
	• Aggregated analytics across all their advisors
	• Optional branded public-facing profile/landing page (white-label)

My Clients/Book of life - Originally scoped as a document storage feature, this has grown into a full client relationship module:
	• Advisor maintains a living record for each client: personal details, policies held, dependants, beneficiaries, life events
	• Clients can optionally access their own "Book of Life" through the public app, viewing their policies, updating personal details, uploading documents
	• Birthday, reviews and renewal reminders for the advisor
	• Emergency contacts visible on the client's profile
	• This becomes the anchor feature that makes Advisory Connect indispensable - it's not just a lead tool anymore, it's a practice management platform with a client portal
